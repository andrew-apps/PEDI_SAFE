# Fever in Infants (0-12 Months): Symptom Checker

## Quick Assessment Guide

### RED FLAGS - Seek Emergency Care Immediately
- Baby is under 3 months with ANY fever (100.4°F/38°C or higher)
- Seizure or convulsion
- Difficulty breathing or rapid breathing
- Blue or gray skin color
- Inconsolable crying for more than 3 hours
- Extremely limp or unresponsive
- Bulging soft spot (fontanelle)
- Purple or blood-red spots on skin that don't fade with pressure
- Stiff neck or body

### ORANGE - Call Pediatrician Now (Same Day)
- Baby 3-6 months with fever above 101°F (38.3°C)
- Baby 6-12 months with fever above 102°F (38.9°C)
- Fever lasting more than 24 hours
- Signs of ear infection (pulling at ear, fussy)
- Rash appearing with fever
- Unusual crying or irritability
- Not feeding well
- Fewer wet diapers than usual

### YELLOW - Call Pediatrician Within 24 Hours
- Low-grade fever (under 101°F) lasting more than 2 days
- Mild cold symptoms with fever
- Recently vaccinated (mild fever expected)
- Acting slightly fussy but able to be comforted

### GREEN - Home Care Appropriate
- Baby over 6 months with low-grade fever
- Playing and feeding normally during fever-free times
- Good fluid intake
- Normal wet diapers
- Can be comforted easily
- No other concerning symptoms

## Assessment Questions

### 1. What is baby's exact age?
- Under 4 weeks: EMERGENCY for any fever
- 1-3 months: URGENT for fever ≥100.4°F
- 3-6 months: Call doctor for fever ≥101°F
- 6-12 months: Monitor, call for fever ≥102°F or concerns

### 2. What is the temperature and how was it taken?
- Rectal is most accurate for infants
- Add 1°F if taken under arm
- Digital thermometers recommended

### 3. How long has baby had fever?
- Just started (under 24 hours): Monitor if no red flags
- 24-48 hours: Consider calling pediatrician
- More than 48-72 hours: Should be evaluated

### 4. How is baby acting?
- Alert and responsive: Good sign
- Feeding normally: Good sign
- Unusually sleepy or hard to wake: Concerning
- Inconsolable or weak cry: Concerning
- Limp or not moving normally: EMERGENCY

### 5. What other symptoms are present?
- Runny nose/mild cough: Common with viral illness
- Vomiting/diarrhea: Watch for dehydration
- Rash: Describe to doctor
- Pulling at ears: Possible ear infection
- Difficulty breathing: EMERGENCY

## Dehydration Warning Signs
- No wet diaper in 8+ hours
- No tears when crying
- Dry mouth and lips
- Sunken soft spot
- Sunken eyes
- Very sleepy or fussy
- **If signs present: Seek medical care**
